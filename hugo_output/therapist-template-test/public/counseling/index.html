Treatment Modalities

EMDR
A research-backed therapy that facilitates the processing of traumatic memories through bilateral stimulation, reducing their lingering emotional charge.
Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT)
Combines mindfulness skills with the practice of self-acceptance, encouraging psychological flexibility and commitment to personal values.
Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
A structured type of talk therapy focused on developing practical skills to manage everyday challenges by changing negative thought patterns.
Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT)
A cognitive-behavioral approach that emphasizes the psychosocial aspects of treatment, teaching skills to manage emotions and improve relationships.
Attachment Based Therapies
Explores how early life experiences and attachment styles influence current relationships and emotional patterns.
IFS or Parts Work
Views the mind as a system of various "parts," helping to heal wounded parts and restore leadership to the core Self.
